Why I Teach

I teach because I want to fundamentally change the way students perceive their own potential. For many, mathematics is viewed as a rigid set of rules where you are either "good at it" or "bad at it."

"My goal is to dismantle that fixed mindset and replace it with a growth mindset through intentional, visual presentation."

When math is presented as a dynamic puzzle rather than rote memorization, students see that their abilities grow through dedication. Mistakes aren't failures; they are crucial data points in the learning process.
